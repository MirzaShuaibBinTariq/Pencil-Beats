Cattle Farming

Cattle farming is an important branch of animal husbandry that focuses on rearing cows and buffaloes for milk, draught work and other useful products. Scientific cattle farming improves milk production, animal health and farmers' income.

🐄 What is Cattle Farming?

Cattle farming is the scientific management of cows and buffaloes for obtaining milk and other useful products. It includes proper breeding, feeding, housing and healthcare of animals.

Modern dairy farming uses improved breeds, balanced nutrition and veterinary care to achieve higher milk production.

🎯 Purpose of Cattle Farming

🥛 Milk Production

The primary objective is to obtain clean, nutritious and high-quality milk for human consumption.

🚜 Draught Animals

Bullocks are used for ploughing fields, transporting goods and performing agricultural work.

🌱 Organic Manure

Cow dung is used as natural manure and for producing biogas, which is an eco-friendly source of energy.

🐂 Breeds of Cattle

Different breeds of cattle are selected according to the purpose of farming, such as milk production or draught work.

🏡 Indigenous Breeds

Native Indian breeds such as Sahiwal, Red Sindhi and Gir are well adapted to local climatic conditions and possess good disease resistance.

🌍 Exotic Breeds

Breeds like Jersey and Holstein Friesian produce large quantities of milk but require better management and care.

🔄 Cross Breeding

Cross breeding combines the desirable qualities of indigenous and exotic breeds to obtain animals with higher milk yield and better adaptability.

🌾 Care and Feeding of Cattle

Proper feeding and care are essential for maintaining the health of cattle and increasing milk production. Dairy animals require a balanced diet containing carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals and sufficient clean drinking water.

🥬 Balanced Diet

A balanced diet includes green fodder, dry fodder, concentrates and mineral mixtures to meet the nutritional requirements of cattle.

💧 Clean Water

Fresh and clean drinking water should always be available, as milk-producing animals require large amounts of water every day.

🍀 Fodder Crops

Common fodder crops include berseem, maize, oats and Napier grass, which provide essential nutrients for healthy growth and milk production.

🏠 Housing of Cattle

A clean, spacious and well-ventilated cattle shed protects animals from extreme weather and diseases while ensuring their comfort.

  • Provide clean and dry flooring.
  • Ensure proper ventilation and sunlight.
  • Protect animals from excessive heat, cold and rain.
  • Clean the shed regularly to prevent infections.
  • Provide adequate space for feeding and resting.

🥛 Hygienic Milking Practices

Milk should be collected under hygienic conditions to maintain its quality and prevent contamination.

🧼 Cleanliness

Wash the udder and hands before milking. Use clean utensils to collect milk safely.

⏰ Regular Milking

Milking should be done at fixed times every day to maintain consistent milk production.

🧊 Safe Storage

Milk should be cooled immediately after collection to maintain freshness and prevent bacterial growth.

💉 Disease Prevention

Healthy cattle produce better quality milk and remain productive for a longer time. Diseases can be prevented by maintaining hygiene, proper nutrition and timely vaccinations.

  • Vaccinate animals regularly.
  • Provide balanced nutrition.
  • Maintain clean surroundings.
  • Isolate sick animals to prevent disease spread.
  • Consult a veterinarian whenever animals show signs of illness.

💡 Remember

Healthy cattle, balanced nutrition, hygienic housing and regular veterinary care are essential for successful dairy farming and high milk production.

📝 JKBOSE Exam Tip

Remember the differences between indigenous and exotic breeds, the components of a balanced diet and the importance of hygienic milking practices. These topics are commonly asked in JKBOSE examinations.

📖 Summary

  • Cattle farming involves the scientific rearing of cows and buffaloes for milk and other useful products.
  • Good breeds, balanced nutrition and proper management increase milk production.
  • Indigenous breeds are hardy and disease-resistant, while exotic breeds produce higher quantities of milk.
  • Clean housing, hygienic milking and adequate drinking water are essential for healthy cattle.
  • Regular vaccination and veterinary care help prevent diseases and improve productivity.
  • Cattle farming provides milk, manure, draught power and income to farmers.
