Revision & Questions

Complete revision of Chapter 4: Improvement in Food Resources. This section contains important points, definitions, MCQs and exam-oriented questions for JKBOSE Class 9 Biology preparation.

📚 Chapter Quick Revision

🌾 Crop Improvement

Crop variety improvement develops better crop varieties with higher yield, disease resistance and improved quality.

🌱 Crop Production

It includes nutrient management, irrigation, manure, fertilisers and modern farming practices.

🛡 Crop Protection

Protection of crops from weeds, pests and diseases using chemical, biological and mechanical methods.

🐄 Animal Husbandry

Scientific management of domestic animals for milk, meat, eggs and other useful products.

🐟 Fish Production

Fish farming and fisheries provide nutritious food and employment opportunities.

🐝 Apiculture

Scientific rearing of honey bees for honey, beeswax and crop pollination.

📖 Important Definitions

  • Crop Variety Improvement: Development of improved crop varieties with desirable characteristics.
  • Manure: Organic substance obtained from plant and animal waste used to improve soil fertility.
  • Fertiliser: Chemical substance rich in nutrients used to increase crop growth.
  • Irrigation: Artificial supply of water to crops at regular intervals.
  • Animal Husbandry: Scientific management of domestic animals.
  • Pisciculture: Breeding and rearing of fish for commercial purposes.
  • Apiculture: Scientific rearing of honey bees for honey production.

1. The process of developing improved crop varieties is called:

A) Irrigation

Answer: B) Crop Variety Improvement

2. Which of the following is a macronutrient?

A) Zinc

3. Growing two or more crops together without a fixed pattern is called:

✍️ Short Answer Questions

Q1. What is crop variety improvement?

Crop variety improvement is the development of improved crop varieties with desirable characteristics like high yield, disease resistance and better quality.

Q2. Write two advantages of manure.

• Improves soil fertility.
• Increases water-holding capacity of soil.

Q3. What is intercropping?

Intercropping is the practice of growing two or more crops together in a definite row pattern on the same field.

Q4. What are weeds?

Weeds are unwanted plants that grow along with cultivated crops and compete for nutrients, water and sunlight.

Q5. Define animal husbandry.

Animal husbandry is the scientific management of domestic animals for obtaining useful products like milk, meat and eggs.

📝 Long Answer Questions

Q1. Explain the methods of crop variety improvement.

Crop variety improvement is done through scientific methods like selection and hybridisation. Scientists select plants with desirable traits and cross different varieties to combine useful characteristics such as high yield, disease resistance and better quality.

Q2. Explain different cropping patterns.

The three main cropping patterns are:

1. Mixed Cropping: Growing two or more crops together on the same land without a fixed pattern.

2. Intercropping: Growing two or more crops in a definite row arrangement.

3. Crop Rotation: Growing different crops one after another in different seasons to maintain soil fertility.

Q3. Describe the importance of animal husbandry.

Animal husbandry helps in the scientific management of livestock. It increases the production of milk, meat and eggs, provides employment and improves the income of farmers. Proper feeding, breeding and healthcare are essential parts of animal husbandry.

Q4. Explain composite fish culture.

Composite fish culture is a method where different species of fish are grown together in the same pond. These fish have different feeding zones, so they do not compete for food. This increases total fish production.

Q5. Explain apiculture and its importance.

Apiculture is the scientific rearing of honey bees for honey and beeswax production. Honey bees also help in pollination of crops, increasing agricultural production and providing additional income to farmers.
