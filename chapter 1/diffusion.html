🌬️ Diffusion

The movement of particles from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration.

Definition

Diffusion is the movement of molecules or particles from an area where they are present in higher concentration to an area where they are present in lower concentration until they are evenly distributed.

How Diffusion Occurs

  1. Particles are crowded in one region.
  2. They move randomly in all directions.
  3. Gradually they spread toward the region of lower concentration.
  4. Eventually, the particles become evenly distributed.

Examples of Diffusion

  • The smell of perfume spreading throughout a room.
  • The aroma of food reaching different parts of a house.
  • Oxygen moving from the lungs into the blood.
  • Carbon dioxide moving from blood into the lungs.

Importance of Diffusion

  • Helps in gaseous exchange.
  • Allows nutrients to move inside cells.
  • Removes waste products from cells.
  • Supports normal cellular activities.

📌 Key Points

  • Diffusion is a passive process; it does not require energy.
  • Particles move from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration.
  • Diffusion continues until the particles are evenly distributed.
  • It occurs in gases, liquids and even solids (very slowly).
  • Diffusion is essential for the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide in living organisms.

📝 Remember

High Concentration → Low Concentration
Diffusion always occurs down the concentration gradient until equilibrium is reached.
